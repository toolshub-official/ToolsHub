Why You Should Remove Metadata From Your Images

Published on May 15, 2023 ‚Ä¢ 5 min read

When you take a photo with your smartphone or digital camera, the image file contains more than just the picture. Hidden within the file is metadata - information about when, where, and how the photo was taken. This metadata, often called EXIF data, can include:

  • GPS coordinates showing exactly where the photo was taken
  • The date and time the photo was captured
  • Camera make and model
  • Camera settings like aperture, shutter speed, and ISO
  • Software used to edit the image

While this information can be useful for photographers organizing their work, it can also pose privacy risks when sharing images online...

Frequently Asked Questions

What types of metadata does this tool remove? ‚ûï

Our tool removes all common types of image metadata including:

  • EXIF data (camera settings, date/time, etc.)
  • GPS location coordinates
  • Camera make and model information
  • Thumbnail previews embedded in the image
  • IPTC and XMP metadata (used by professional photographers)
  • Any other proprietary metadata formats
Is my image data secure? ‚ûï

Yes! All image processing happens directly in your web browser. Your images are never uploaded to our servers or any third-party services. This ensures maximum privacy and security for your sensitive photos.

What image formats are supported? ‚ûï

We currently support the most common image formats:

  • JPEG/JPG (best support for metadata removal)
  • PNG
  • WebP

We're working to add support for additional formats in future updates.

Will removing metadata affect image quality? ‚ûï

No, the visual quality of your image will remain exactly the same. Metadata removal only affects the hidden information stored with the image, not the actual image pixels.
